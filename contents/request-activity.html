Recurring Schedule

Add activity

    Add something to the recurring schedule

    If you’d like a weekly or regular activity listed in the left-hand schedule—book club, cards, walking group, whatever—fill this out and send it to the event coordinator. One-time parties or special nights belong on Submit Event instead.

    • You’ll pick which days it usually meets and what time folks show up.
    • One main photo helps neighbors recognize the activity—we’ll bundle it with your message.
    • When you tap send, your device opens email (or Share) with the details filled in, same idea as the event form.
    About the activity

    The name neighbors will see on the schedule—keep it short and clear.

    A few sentences are plenty: who it’s for, what to bring, if there’s a signup, and so on.

    Pick the closest spot. If it’s somewhere else, choose Other and describe it below.

    Name the room or area so people know where to go.

    When it meets

    These activities repeat on the same day(s) each week. Check every day that usually applies—you can always ask the coordinator to tweak it later.

    Use the time picker your device shows—most people pick something like 7:00 p.m.

    Repeats on

    Tap each day this activity usually meets—we need at least one day checked to add it.

    Extras (optional)

    If you want, add a few words separated by commas—like cards, seniors, beginners. Skip this if you’re not sure.

    Photos

    Please add one main photo for the activity. Extra shots are welcome if you have them—they ride along when you send your request.

    For email, smaller files send more easily: about 4 MB per photo and 16 MB total is a comfortable target. We’ll give you a heads-up if your picks run bigger than that.

    How can the coordinator reach you?

    Optional, but helpful if something in your request needs a quick question.

    Point of contact

    If someone sees this activity on the schedule and wants to know more, the coordinator can list you so neighbors know who to ask.

    Ready to send?

    Your email app should open with the coordinator’s address and your notes ready to go. Photos ride along automatically on many phones and tablets.

    On some computers a single file may download first—if you see that, just attach it to the email before you hit send. Nothing sneaky; it’s your request and pictures in one package.
