    Pétanque

    If bocce ball and a Parisian sidewalk had a low-stress baby, you'd get pétanque: roll or lob metal boules toward a wee target (the jack, a.k.a. cochonnet), nudge yours closer, or gleefully bump a rival’s ball off its meditation cushion. No running, no nets—just strategy, sunshine, and trash talk on Monday and Thursday mornings in the Northwest corner of the park.

    About this activity

    Pétanque is a classic French boules sport: players take turns throwing or rolling boules on an outdoor court, aiming to finish with their balls nearest the jack—or to politely relocate someone else's. It scores like the games you already know from bocce ball: think “closest to the little ball wins the bragging rights,” with room for finesse shots and heroic recoveries.

    Newcomers catch on fast; old hands get to show off. Bring a hat and water. Questions or need loaner gear info? Email the event coordinator.
