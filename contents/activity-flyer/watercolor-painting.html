    Seasonal activity

    Watercolor Painting

    Bring a personal painting—or a blank page and a small idea. This circle is built around watercolor: soft washes, lifted highlights, glazing a shadow in two quiet passes, or chasing a landscape, floral, or still-life corner you actually care about. You set the subject; neighbors bring encouragement, troubleshooting, and the occasional “try a drier brush there.”

    About this activity

    Watercolor is forgiving of curiosity: sketch light, lay a wash, let it dry, then decide if that tree needs one more cool layer. People work on different pieces side by side—someone mixing greens for palms, someone else trying wet-on-wet sky for the first time—so you pick up ideas without giving up your own plan.

    Bring your usual supplies if you have them; if you are new, ask what paper and a starter pan set the group recommends. Chair: Judy Norton. Stop by on a Monday morning, or email the event coordinator for contact info or schedule questions.
